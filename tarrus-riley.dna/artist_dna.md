# Artist DNA — Tarrus Riley
*Source of truth for all creative output. Website, cover art, merch, brand extension all derive from here.*

---

## Identity

**Artist name:** Tarrus Riley
**Domain:** tarrusrileyja.com
**Genre / subgenre:** Roots reggae / lover's rock / conscious reggae
**Active since:** 2006

---

## Axis 1 — The Word

**The Word:** Roots

*Why this word and not another:* "Roots" carries Tarrus Riley's entire identity simultaneously — his Rastafarian faith, his father's legacy (Jimmy Riley), his sonic return to foundational reggae, and his connection to the earth and people. He doesn't chase trends. He goes deeper into where the music came from. Every album, every hit, every collaboration circles back to this. "Roots" is not a genre label — it is his operating system.

---

## Axis 2 — The Frequency

**Primary frequency:** Spiritual uplift — the feeling of being held and elevated at the same time. His music makes people feel seen and cared for.

**Secondary frequency:** Warmth — not happiness, warmth. The specific feeling of something that has been lived in and loved. Like a room that remembers good things.

**What it is NOT:** Aggressive, urgent, anxious, cold, ironic. Tarrus Riley's music has no detachment. It is fully present and fully sincere every time.

---

## Axis 3 — The Colour Truth

**The feeling:** The last hour of daylight in the hills of Jamaica. Warm, golden, unhurried. The kind of light that makes everything look like it was always supposed to be there.

**Translated to colour family:** Warm amber and aged gold as primaries. Deep forest green as the anchor (roots, earth, Rasta connection). Cognac leather and burnt sienna as mid tones. Off-white linen as the light.

**What colour does NOT belong to this artist:** Cold blues, stark white, neon of any kind, grey modernism. Anything that feels like a city at night rather than hills at dusk.

---

## Axis 4 — The Texture

**The texture:** Worn smooth leather. The kind that started rough and became beautiful through use. Warm wood grain — not polished lacquer, real wood with marks in it. The texture of a record sleeve that has been handled a thousand times.

**How this applies to the site:** Card backgrounds use warm dark tones with a slight vignette — not flat black. Image overlays are warm amber gradients, not cool. Any grain texture on the hero is fine film grain, not digital noise. Surfaces feel tactile, considered, used.

---

## Axis 5 — The Tempo

**Music tempo feel:** Mid-pace, steady, deliberate. Reggae one-drop rhythm — the beat falls on the third, unhurried, inevitable. Nothing rushed. Nothing wasted.

**Site animation tempo:** Slow and intentional. 0.8–1.0s transitions. Easing curves that decelerate gracefully — content arrives rather than appears. Nothing snaps or cuts.

**Scroll behaviour:** Free scroll, smooth. No section snapping — the experience should flow like a song, not jump like a playlist.

---

## Axis 6 — The Tension

**The tension:** Sacred vs sensual. Tarrus Riley is deeply spiritual — Rasta, conscious, faith-forward. He is also one of the premier lover's rock artists in reggae — his love songs are intimate, physical, tender. These two things exist fully in the same person with no contradiction. The tension is that both are completely sincere.

**How this tension shows up visually:** Reverent layout with warm, intimate photography treatment. Typography that has both authority (the spiritual) and softness (the sensual). Gold that reads as both sacred and luxurious. The site feels like a place of worship that also feels like home.

---

## Axis 7 — The Cultural Anchor

**The anchor:** Rastafarian spirituality filtered through the reggae lineage. Tarrus Riley is Jimmy Riley's son — he carries the weight of the music's history personally, not academically. The sound system culture, the yard sessions, the conscious lyrics as spiritual practice.

**Visual manifestation:** Lion imagery is earned here (not generic Rasta decoration). Natural textures — wood, earth, linen. Photography that treats the artist as a figure of gravity rather than a celebrity. No flashy or commercial visual language. The culture is the decoration.

---

## Axis 8 — The Typography Register

**Voice quality:** Warm and authoritative — the voice of someone who has earned the right to speak slowly and be listened to. Never shouts. Never needs to.

**Heading font direction:** Flowing italic serif or elegant transitional display. "Riley" in cursive is the brand anchor. The heading font should feel handcrafted, not manufactured. Cormorant Garamond, Playfair Display, or Syncopate (current — acceptable but could be warmer).

**Body font direction:** Warm humanist sans — Space Grotesk (current, good fit). Readable, human, not clinical.

**Letter spacing energy:** Normal to slightly wide — deliberate, unhurried. Never tight. Tight spacing belongs to urgency. Tarrus Riley has no urgency.

---

## Axis 9 — The Era Reference

**Era reference:** 1970s roots Jamaica — the Studio One and Channel One era. The visual grammar of that period: warm photography, natural light, organic layouts, earth tones. Not retro as a style choice — retro as a living inheritance.

**Visual elements from that era to pull:** Warm film grain on photography. Sepia-adjacent colour grading (amber, not grey). Layout that breathes — generous padding. Hand-lettered-feeling type even when it is digital. Natural fibres as texture reference.

**What era to actively avoid:** Any post-2010 digital minimalism. Cold Scandinavian design. Corporate clean. Anything that looks like it was made by someone who has never been to Jamaica.

---

## Axis 10 — The Motion

**Stage/performance energy:** Tarrus Riley performs with complete stillness at his centre. He does not move aggressively — he holds ground and lets the music move the room. His presence is gravitational, not kinetic.

**Site motion character:** Slow coastal drift. Content arrives like the tide — inevitable, unhurried, with weight. Nothing jumps. Nothing flashes.

**Hero section motion:** Parallax — artist photo or warm background video with a slow drift. The hero should feel like it's breathing.

**Scroll reveal style:** Fade up, slow. 0.8s, ease-out. Staggered on grouped elements (e.g. track list rows). The reveal feels like a curtain lifting, not a door opening.

### Typography Stack

- **Heading:** Syncopate (current) or Cormorant Garamond Italic — Bold — 4xl to 9xl range
- **Body:** Space Grotesk — Regular/Medium — 14–16px
- **Accent / mono:** Space Grotesk Mono or similar — 10–11px, tracked wide

### Animation Spec

- Transition duration: 800ms
- Easing: cubic-bezier(0.16, 1, 0.3, 1) — smooth deceleration
- Scroll reveal: fade up, 30px travel, 800ms, once
- Hero motion: slow parallax drift (Y axis only, 20% travel on scroll)

### Cover Art Direction

A Tarrus Riley cover in this DNA: warm amber and forest green as the dominant tones. The artist photographed in natural light — dusk or late afternoon, outdoors or near a window. Film grain overlay at 15–20% opacity. The typography uses the Riley cursive wordmark large, anchored at the bottom third. No hard edges — everything bleeds into everything else through gradient and light. The image feels like a memory of Jamaica more than a photograph of it. Background: earth, hills, or a dimly lit room with warm light sources. Never a white background. Never a studio drop. The cover should feel like it could have been made in 1976 or 2026 with no way to tell which.
